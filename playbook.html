FILE 01

The Founding Myth

It began, as all great movements do, during a power cut.

A comedian, a chai-stall philosopher, and an out-of-work meme editor were sitting in the dark, swatting at a cockroach that simply would not die. Somebody said it out loud: this insect is more reliable than any party we've ever voted for. It shows up. It survives. It doesn't pretend it isn't in your house. And unlike the manifesto, it actually delivers — every single night, on schedule, in your kitchen.

So they founded a party in the cockroach's honour. Not to mock the voter — never the voter — but to mock the grand machinery that has perfected the art of promising the moon while delivering the dark. The Cockroach Janata Party (CJP) is a joke. We say so openly, on every poster, in every speech. And that single act of honesty makes us, statistically, the most truthful organisation on the ballot.

"They lie and pretend they don't. We're a joke and we admit it. Decide for yourself who's more trustworthy."

This playbook is the operating manual for that joke — and for the deeply serious point hiding inside it.

FILE 03

The Honesty Doctrine

The whole engine. The jokes are the bodywork; this is the motor.

Principle 01

Radical honesty through absurdity

We never claim to be serious. The absurdity is the proof of sincerity — a clown can't be caught lying about being a clown. Our founding line is printed on everything: "At least we admit it."

Principle 02

The promise to break promises

"We promise to break every promise. This is our manifesto's only guarantee." When we keep it, we become the only party whose word held. When we accidentally help someone, it's a delightful bonus.

Principle 03

Impossibly sincere, perfectly deadpan

Every promise is delivered with the straight face of a budget speech. The gap between the calm tone and the insane content is where the satire lives — and where the real critique lands.

Principle 04

Never punch the voter

The target is always the system and the generic political class, never a community, never the citizen who's been let down. The voter is the hero of this story; the cockroach merely volunteers to serve.

The Twist (keep this sacred)If we actually win, we govern competently, humbly, and cleanly — because the entire point is that the clowns turned out to be the only adults in the room. The bit that elevates a meme into satire: the joke party keeps the lights on.
FILE 04

Manifesto of Impossible Promises

Read in the soothing monotone of someone reading a railway timetable.

1

A Pothole-Free Nation

Achieved overnight by officially reclassifying every pothole as a "Free Public Rainwater-Harvesting & Community Swimming Facility." Drowning is reframed as "aquatic enrichment."

2

The Rupee, Stronger Than the Dollar

We will print the word STRONG on every note. Confidence is a currency; we are simply being transparent about the methodology.

3

Every Flooded City Becomes the Venice of the East

Monsoon waterlogging is a tourism asset. Gondola licences will be issued to anyone who already owns a thermocol sheet and a sense of optimism.

4

Antakshari Replaces Question Hour

Parliamentary debate, but if your answer doesn't begin with the last letter of the previous minister's lie, you lose your turn. Far more productive than the current format.

5

Free 24×7 Electricity

Delivered by redefining "darkness" as "complimentary stargazing." Every load-shedding becomes a state-sponsored astronomy programme. Look up — that's a feature.

6

Zero Unemployment

All citizens are hereby reclassified as "self-employed influencers of their own destiny." Statistics improve immediately and at no cost to anyone except meaning.

7

The Fastest Trains in the World

By relabelling all delays as "extended scenic appreciation windows." A train that never arrives can never be late. This is just mathematics.

8

An End to Traffic Forever

We will simply stop measuring it. What is not measured does not exist. Your three-hour commute is now a "mobile meditation retreat."

9

Clean Air for All

The AQI app will be updated to only display encouraging adjectives — "cosy," "characterful," "rustic." The sky stays grey; your outlook turns sunny.

10

And, Finally — We Will Break Every One of These

This is the only promise we guarantee. We are, after all, the only party honest enough to put the broken promise in the manifesto itself.

A two-front war: the street corner and the smartphone.

Ground Game

Nukkad Natak Brigade

Roving street plays in chawls, mandis and bus stands. A performer in a roach costume "debates" an empty chair labelled the Hot Air Party — and wins, because the chair makes no promises it can't keep either, but at least the roach is funny about it.

Air Game

The Meme Army

A decentralised volunteer corps armed with templates, AI tools and a shared sense of humour. The full battle-kit lives in the companion Content Pack — 60+ ready ideas for memes, posters, reels and songs. (See the second dossier.)

Earned Media

The Symbol Reveal

Treat the cockroach symbol like a movie launch: teaser, countdown, a dramatic "first look." Make the press cover an insect with the gravity of a coronation. The absurdity is the story.

Door-to-Door

The Honest Canvass

Volunteers knock and open with: "Hi, we won't fix your road. But we won't pretend we will, either. Tea?" Disarming honesty converts more cynics than any guarantee.

FILE 08

Coalition Rules

We will only share power with those who have proven their soul. (Borrowed, with respect, from the party that demanded everyone watch The Wire.)

Test 01

The Wedding Trial

No alliance with anyone who hasn't sat through a full three-day Indian wedding without once checking their phone. If you can survive that, you can survive coalition arithmetic.

Test 02

The May Power Cut

Candidates must endure a four-hour daytime power cut in peak summer, no AC, no inverter, only a hand-fan and dignity. This reveals character faster than any affidavit.

Test 03

The Auto Negotiation

You must successfully negotiate an autorickshaw fare at 2am in the rain, by the meter, without raising your voice. Diplomacy is diplomacy.

Hard LineWe test the soul, not the CV. A spotless résumé means nothing; a person who returned the extra change at the kirana store means everything.
FILE 09

If We Actually Win

The punchline that isn't a punchline. We govern like the adults nobody expected.

Keep the lights on, literally

The first act is the least funny one: fix the boring things — water, power, the road outside the school. Competence is the most subversive joke a joke party can tell.

Open the books

Every rupee in, every rupee out, published in plain language a chai-stall can read. We were honest about being a joke; we'll be honest about the budget.

Hire people smarter than us

The mascot delegates. We staff offices with the competent and the kind, and stay out of their way. Humility is policy.

Never start believing our own legend

The day a CJP minister gives a speech about their own greatness, the party disbands itself on the spot. Self-seriousness is the only resignation letter we accept.

Hand back the chappal

When the work is done, we step aside without clinging to the chair. The cockroach doesn't need the kitchen to be its own — it just needs the kitchen to be okay.
